## üìò How the Game Begins

The session unfolds in a structured, story-driven way. Below are the core steps that kick off every case:

### 1) The DM sets the scene
The story opens with a psychological scenario or clinical case: a patient, a room, a strange mental landscape, or a half-remembered memory.  
From the very first moment, players are immersed in a narrative and clinical atmosphere created by the DM.

### 2) Roles are assigned
Each player receives their role card and role-specific limitations.  
Role cards contain confidential clues and behavioral constraints that shape how each player can interact during the game.

### 3) The puzzle is activated
The DM reveals an initial set of clues. Players then use their roles to:

- **Gather information** from available sources and role interactions  
- **Connect clues** and look for patterns across fragments  
- **Analyze the patient's behavior or the presented environment** from their role perspective  
- **Form step-by-step hypotheses** and iteratively refine diagnostic reasoning

As the session progresses, the DM will release additional clues, allow dice-based attempts to unlock memories or details, and guide the pacing so the team can gradually assemble the full case.
